## Core positioning
TennisLevel is not a tennis content platform.
It is a training decision routing layer for amateur players: catch vague colloquial inputs, narrow and judge first, then route users to the most relevant content and plan so they can find the single next thing to practice.

## Product boundaries
- Not a content-abundance answer: the scarce value is narrowing, judgment, positioning, and routing.
- Not a replacement for Bilibili/YouTube: TennisLevel sits before those platforms to reduce direction entropy.
- Not a coach replacement or training executor: it helps users decide what to focus on next.

## Experience backbone
Primary interaction flow:
1. Capture a vague or colloquial user issue.
2. Narrow into actionable sub-directions (technical, movement, mental, context).
3. Route to one primary current focus.
4. Recommend one short rationale and a small set of relevant resources.
5. Offer a concrete next-step plan.

## Detailed standards kept in docs (not AGENTS)
- Button size, information density, above-the-fold block count, and cognitive load.
- Operability for different ages, devices, and reading speeds.
- zh/en semantic alignment and translation consistency.
- Creator and problem-tag taxonomy governance.
- Concrete writing rules for fallback, explanation, and plan text.

## Routing-first quality checks
- Is there exactly one primary next-step focus?
- Does the output clearly separate technical/movement/mental/context signals?
- Is confidence wording proportional to available evidence?
- Is the plan executable (order, amount, done criteria), not a renamed content list?
- Are recommendations relevant and direct-source compliant?
